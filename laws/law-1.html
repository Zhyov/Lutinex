Sanctioned: May 16 of 2024.

The creator and King of the Lutinex Nation enacts with force of

Law:

Article 1 : The publication of the official text of the National Constitution (sanctioned in 22/9/2023 with the reforms of 23/9/2023, 25/9/2023, 17/10/2023, 26/10/2023, 30/10/2023, 28/4/2024 and 16/5/2024) is ordered, which is transcribed below:

CONSTITUTION OF THE LUTINEX NATION

PREAMBLE

I, the creator and King of the Lutinex Nation, wrote this text, which is inspired on the Argentinian Constitution, with the objective of: having a unified nation, having justice in our country, having peace in our country, having freedom in our country, to live even better and to protect each other. This Constitution is for every Lutinian and for all people who want to live in our country.

FIRST PART

Chapter One

Declarations, rights and guarantees

Article 1 : The Lutinex Nation is a Federal Talsampsic (Republican Representative with a seperate Absolute Monarch above the Representative President/s) Republic, this being the Kingdom of its United Provinces.

Article 2 : The Lutinex Nation's official languages are: English, Ryabaj (written as Rjabázh), Spanish (written as Español) and Toxer (written as Tósker), being English the one used officialy inside the government.

Article 3 : The capital of the country is the City of Uron in the province of Navioc. The national authorities live in the capital city.

Article 4 : The national treasury is the money available to cover the national government's expenditures. The money in the National Treasury comes from: sale or rent of land in the country, taxes on things going in and out of the country, taxes paid by the population and loans that the government borrows when the money from taxes is not enough. Taxes should be equal: the more you have, the more you pay.

Article 5 : Each province has its own constitution. Provincial constitutions must: respect the representative and republican form of government, respect what the national constitution says, ensure their own judicial power and organise their municipal system. The national government ensures each province the exercise of its own institutions.

Article 6 : The national government may intervene in a province to: secure the republican form of government, repel external attack, ensure that authorities threatened or expelled by rebellion or invasion from another province are restored to office.

Article 7 : The acts of government of one province are valid in all provinces. The judicial proceedings of one province are valid in all provinces.

Article 8 : Citizens of one province are citizens in the other provinces. Citizens of one province have the same rights as citizens of other provinces. A person arrested in one province must be handed over to the authorities of another province which claims him or her for trial.

Article 9 : In the Lutinex Nation there are customs offices at the borders of the country. The national Congress creates the taxes that are charged on the country's borders.

Article 10 : Goods can move freely within the country. Goods can be manufactured in the country or brought from other countries. Goods and animals moved from one province to another are not taxed.

Article 11 : A new province may be formed by the union or division of other provinces. The legislative powers of those provinces must agree to the formation of the new province. The national Congress must agree to the formation of the new province.

Article 12 : The people of this country have the right to: work, sail and trade, petition the government, walk freely throughout the country, enter and leave the country, stay in the country, say and publish what they think, use and decide what to do with their things, associate to do good and useful things, practise the religion of their choice, teach and learn.

Article 13 : In the Lutinex Nation all persons are free. In the Lutinex Nation slavery is prohibited.

Article 14 : In this country there are titles of nobility. We are all equal before the law. We all have to pay taxes. We can all have a job according to our our abilities.

Article 15 : Property is immutable. No one can restrict the use or take away our things unless: a judge decides it because a law says so or the government decides it because we have something necessary for everyone. The government must pay us if it takes something from us for the benefit of everyone. Every author or inventor owns his or her work for as long as the law says.

Article 16 : For someone to convict you, there has to be a law that says that what you did is a crime. No one can arrest you without a judge's order. They cannot convict you without a trial. You have rights even if you are charged in a trial. You have the right to have a lawyer defend you in a trial. No one can force you to say things against you in a trial. No one can enter your home without your permission.

Article 17 : One's private acts cannot be judged by judges unless they offend: public order, the common good, the rights of others. You may do anything that is not forbidden by law. You must not do anything forbidden by law.

Article 18 : Foreigners living in our country have the right to: work, to own things, buy and sell things, to walk freely throughout the country, keep their religious beliefs, marry and make wills. Foreigners can be Lutinian citizens if they: live for one consecutive month in the country, or render services to the Lutinex Nation.

Article 19 : We must all defend our country. We must all defend this Constitution. Our duty as Lutinians is to defend our country and this Constitution. The people who chose to be Lutinians are not obliged to defend our country.

Article 20 : In our country only elected representatives of the people can govern. Any group of people or armed force that rebels against the government commits a crime.

Article 21 : The government declares a state of siege when something very serious happens in a part of the country: an internal crisis or an attack from outside. The state of siege is to defend the constitution. The government can apply security measures during a state of siege. The government cannot apply sentences during a state of siege. A state of siege suspends some civil rights and guarantees. The government can arrest people or take them to another part of the country during a state of siege. People can leave the country if they do not want to be arrested or taken to another part of the country during a state of siege.

Article 22 : The legislature is responsible for making and changing laws. The legislature has to establish trial by jury.

Article 23 : Regulations of laws may not modify the rights recognised in this Constitution.

Article 24 : The legislature cannot give the president more power than he already has. The legislatures of the provinces cannot give the governors of the provinces more power than they already have.

Article 25 : The Constitution may be changed in whole or in parts. The need for changes to the Constitution must be declared by the national Congress with a special majority. The Constituent Convention is the body that will make changes to the Constitution.

Article 26 : The most important laws in our country are: the Constitution, the laws passed by the national Congress and international treaties. Other laws that are created in the country must respect what the Constitution says.

Article 27 : The National Congress may not make laws that restrict the freedom of the press.

Article 28 : The Constitution lists declarations, rights and guarantees. There may be rights and guarantees that are not in the Constitution. Rights and guarantees that are not in the Constitution may arise from: the principle of sovereignty of the people or the republican form of government.

Article 29 : Federal judges cannot be provincial judges. A federal government employee working in a province has no legal domicile in that province.

Article 30 : Our country can be named: Lutinex Republic, United Provinces of Lutinex, Lutinex Kingdom, Kingdom of the United Provinces of Lutinex and Federal Kingdom of the United Provinces of Lutinex. In the laws, Lutinex Nation is used to name our country.

SECOND CHAPTER

New rights and guarantees

Article 31 : A coup d'état occurs when political power is violently seized. Disrespecting the law and damaging the state is a very serious crime. If a coup takes place: the constitution remains valid and the acts of the usurper government are invalid. Persons who participate in a coup d'état: will be tried and convicted, will serve their full sentence, will not be pardoned and will never be allowed to hold public office All citizens have the right to resist a coup d'état. People who commit serious crimes against the state and enrich themselves by doing so harm the state. The national Congress has to make a law that says how officials should behave.

Article 32 : Citizens have political rights. Voting is a political right. All citizens have the right to vote. No vote is worth more than any other. No citizen is obliged to say for whom he or she votes. Voting is compulsory. Citizens have the right to be elected as representatives of the people. You have to win an election to be a representative of the people. Our representatives must be men and women.

Article 33 : A group of people who share political ideas can form a political party. Political parties are very important in the democratic system. People who want to represent us as rulers must stand for election through political parties. The Constitution guarantees the organisation and functioning of political parties. Political parties cannot be against the constitution. Political parties must say or report where their money comes from and what they are going to do with their money.

Article 33 : Citizens can present bills for the national Congress to make a law. Citizens must present the bill in the Chamber of Deputies. The national Congress has 1 month to analyse the bill. The national Congress must make a law that says what are the requirements to present a bill. Citizens cannot present a bill to change: the Constitution, international treaties, criminal laws, laws that establish taxes or regulate the national budget.

Article 34 : The national Congress can call a referendum on a draft law. The President cannot prevent a referendum from taking place. A vote in favour of a bill submitted to popular consultation automatically makes it law. The president and the national Congress can also call a referendum on a very important issue. Popular consultations can be mandatory or not. The national Congress decides how the referendum is to be held. Representatives must respect the people's decision when the vote is compulsory. Representatives can disregard the people's decision when the vote is not compulsory.

Article 35 : We all have the right to live in a healthy environment. We must all take care of the environment. If we damage the environment we have to fix the damage. The state must create laws and educate people to protect and conserve the environment. The state has to take care that natural resources are used properly. The provinces must also help to take care of the environment. It is forbidden to bring hazardous waste into the country from another country.

Article 36 : Consumers and service users have the right to have: their health taken care of, the things they buy and use are safe. Consumers and service users also have the right to: receive information about what they buy and use, choose what they want, pay a fair price, be treated fairly. Authorities must: protect the rights of consumers and users, educate about the rights and obligations of consumers and users, defend the freedom of choice of consumers and users, defend the freedom of businesses to offer a thing or service, ensure the quality of public services, promote the creation of associations that defend the rights of consumers and users. The laws must ensure: the prevention and solution of problems in consumer relations, the proper functioning of public services, the participation of the provinces and of consumer and user associations in the entities that control the proper functioning of public services.

Article 37 : People can ask for a speedy trial called amparo when: their rights are harmed or threatened, their guarantees are harmed or threatened. Amparo can be requested by: the Ombudsman, associations that protect people's rights, the person affected. There is damage or threat to rights and guarantees when: there is discrimination, the environment is not protected, users and consumers are not protected, a right shared with other persons is attacked. Individuals can also ask for a fast-track amparo when their personal data are: not protected, used for reporting purposes. Individuals can seek correction of their public or private data when: it is untrue, its disclosure may harm them. Journalists can disclose information without saying how they got it. People can ask for a speedy trial called habeas corpus when: their physical liberty is affected, they are treated badly while in detention. Habeas corpus can be requested by: the person concerned or any other person. Judges always have to resolve the habeas corpus trial immediately.

SECOND PART

NATIONAL AUTHORITIES

FIRST TITLE

FEDERAL GOVERNMENT

FIRST SECTION

OF THE LEGISLATIVE POWER

Article 38 : The Lutinian Constitution is a written law. The Lutinian Constitution organises us politically as a country. A politically organised country is a State. The Lutinian state is a republic. A republic is a State that separates and distributes political power. In our country political power is divided into three: Executive Power: president, Legislative Power: deputies and senators, Judicial Power: judges. The authorities of the nation are: the king, the president, the deputies and senators, the judges. The second part of the Lutinian Constitution speaks of our authorities.

FIRST CHAPTER

Of the Chamber of Deputies

Article 39 : The national Congress consists of the Chamber of Deputies and the Chamber of Senators. The Chamber of Deputies is one part of the legislative branch. The Chamber of Senators is another part of the legislative branch. Deputies and Senators are authorities of the nation. The legislative branch makes and changes laws. The national Congress can approve or reject international treaties. The national Congress has approved several international human rights treaties. International human rights treaties have the same importance as the Lutinex Constitution. Deputies and Senators cannot do what the President does. Deputies and Senators cannot do what judges do.

Article 40 : The deputies are representatives of the people of the provinces and the city of Uron. Provinces with more inhabitants have more deputies. Deputies are elected by the people. Deputies serve for 2 months. There are 6 deputies in the Chamber of Deputies. Deputies must: be a Lutinian citizen for more than 2 months and be from the province they represent.

SECOND CHAPTER

Of the Senate

Article 41 : Senators are representatives of the people of the provinces and the city of Uron. Senators are elected by the people in elections. Senators serve for 3 months. There are 6 senators in the Senate Chamber. In the House of Senators there is 1 senator for each province and 1 senator for the city of Uron. Senators must: be Lutinian citizens for more than 3 months and be from the province they represent.

Article 42 : Laws tell us what we must do and what is forbidden. The legislative branch makes and changes laws. Laws are discussed in the Chamber of Deputies and the Chamber of Senators. Laws are passed in the Chamber of Deputies and in the Chamber of Senators. Laws cannot be against our fundamental rights.

SECOND SECTION

OF THE EXECUTIVE POWER

Article 43 : The king is the maximum authority of the nation. The king is above the law. There is only one king, which is the creator of the Lutinex Nation.

Article 44 : The president is an authority of the nation. The president is the head of state. A state is a politically organised country. The people choose the president by voting in elections. The president is in office for 2 months. The president must: be a Lutinian and be a Lutinian citizen for more than 3 months. The executive branch includes the vice president, the chief of staff and ministers. The vice-president replaces the president when: the president is ill or the president is travelling. The chief of staff assists the president in the administration of the country, organises meetings with ministers and the legislature. The ministers are in charge of specific issues: justice, security, education and health. The president makes decisions about the affairs of the country. The president's decisions must be good for all of us. The president must respect the law. The president's decisions cannot be against our fundamental rights. The president cannot do what judges do. The president cannot do what deputies and senators do.

THIRD SECTION

OF THE JUDICIAL POWER

Article 45 : Judges are authorities of the nation. Judges decide disputes according to the law. Judges must respect the law. Judges' decisions can be reviewed by other judges. Judges' decisions can be reviewed by the Supreme Court of Justice of the Nation. The highest court in the country is the Supreme Court of Justice of the Nation. Judges cannot do what the President does. Judges cannot do what deputies and senators do.

SECOND TITLE

PROVINCE GOVERNMENTS

Article 46 : The provinces retain all power not delegated to the national government. Each province elects its governor, deputies, senators and civil servants. Provinces may create regions for economic and social development. Provinces can sign international treaties as long as they do not affect: the country's foreign policy, the powers delegated to the national government and public credit. The provinces have dominion over the natural resources of their region. Provinces can sign agreements to: improve the administration of justice and develop the economy of each region. A province cannot declare war on another province. Conflicts between provinces shall be resolved by the Supreme Court of Justice of the Nation.
