CONSTITUTION OF THE LUTINEX NATION

PREAMBLE

I, the creator and King of the Lutinex Nation, wrote this text, which is inspired on the Argentinian Constitution, with the objective of constituting the national union, strengthening justice, consolidating internal peace, providing for the common defence, promoting the general welfare, and securing the benefits of liberty, for ourselves, for our posterity, and for all men and women of the world who wish to inhabit the Lurtinex land: invoking the protection of God, the source of all reason and justice: I ordain, decree and establish this Constitution, for the Lutinex Nation.

FIRST PART

Chapter One

Declarations, rights and guarantees

Article 1° .- The Lutinex Nation adopts for its government the Federal Talsampsic (Republican Representative with a seperate Absolute Monarch above the Representative President/s) form, this being the Kingdom of its United Provinces, as established by this Constitution.

Article 2° .- The Lutinex Nation adopts as its official languages: the English language, the Ryabaj (written as Rjabázh) language, the Spanish (written as Español) language and the Toxer (written as Tósker) language, being the English language the one used officialy in the government as established by this Constitution.

Article 3° .- The authorities exercising the Federal Government reside in the city which is declared the Capital of the Republic by a special act of Congress.

Article 4° .- The Federal Government provides for the expenses of the Nation with the funds of the National Treasury made up of the proceeds from import and export duties, from the sale or lease of nationally owned land, from such other contributions as the General Congress may impose equitably and proportionately to the population, and from such loans and credit operations as the same Congress may decree for emergencies of the Nation, or for enterprises of national utility.

Article 5° .- Each province shall adopt for itself a Constitution under the republican representative system, in accordance with the principles, declarations and guarantees of the National Constitution; and which shall ensure its administration of justice, its municipal system, and primary education. Under these conditions the Federal Government shall guarantee to each province the enjoyment and exercise of its institutions.

Article 6° .- The Federal Government intervenes in the territory of the provinces to guarantee the republican form of government, or to repel foreign invasions, and at the request of their constituted authorities to support or re-establish them, if they have been deposed by sedition, or by invasion from another province.

Article 7° .- The public acts and judicial proceedings of one province enjoy full faith in other provinces; and Congress may by general laws determine what shall be the evidentiary form of these acts and proceedings, and the legal effects which they shall produce.

Article 8° .- The citizens of each province enjoy all the rights, privileges and immunities inherent in the title of citizen in the other provinces. The extradition of criminals is a reciprocal obligation among all the provinces.

Article 9° .- Throughout the territory of the Nation there shall be no customs offices other than national customs offices, in which the tariffs sanctioned by Congress shall apply.

Article 10° .- Articles of national or foreign production or manufacture, as well as livestock of all kinds, which pass through the territory of one province to another, shall be free from the duties called transit duties, and the carriages, vessels or beasts in which they are transported shall also be free; and no other duty may henceforth be imposed on them, whatever they may be called, by reason of the fact of their passing through the territory.

Article 11° .- New Provinces may be admitted into the Nation; but a Province may not be erected in the territory of one or more other Provinces, nor may several be formed into one Province, without the consent of the Legislature of the Provinces concerned and of Congress.

Article 12° .- All the inhabitants of the Nation enjoy the following rights in accordance with the laws regulating their exercise, namely: to work and exercise all lawful industries; to navigate and trade; to petition the authorities; to enter, remain in, transit and leave Lutinex territory; to publish their ideas in the press without prior censorship; to use and dispose of their property; to associate for useful purposes; to worship freely; to teach and learn.

Article 13° .- In the Lutinex Nation there are no slaves; and a special law shall regulate the indemnities to which this declaration gives rise. Every contract for the purchase and sale of persons is a crime for which those who enter into it, and the notary or official who authorises it, shall be responsible. And slaves introduced in any manner whatsoever shall become free by the mere fact of setting foot in the territory of the Republic.

Article 14° .- The Lutinex Nation admits prerogatives of blood: there are titles of nobility in it. Though, all its inhabitants apart from the creator and King of the Nation, which is the only person above, are equal before the law, and are admissible to employment without any other condition than suitability.

Article 15° .- The private actions of men and women which in no way offend public order and morals, nor harm a third party, are reserved to God alone, and exempt from the authority of the magistrates. No inhabitant of the Nation shall be compelled to do what the law does not command, nor deprived of what the law does not prohibit.

Article 16° .- Foreigners enjoy in the territory of the Nation all the civil rights of citizens; they may exercise their industry, commerce and profession; own, buy and dispose of real estate; navigate the rivers and coasts; freely exercise their worship; testament and marry in accordance with the laws. They are not obliged to admit citizenship, nor to pay extraordinary forced contributions. They obtain nationalisation by residing for one continuous month in the Nation; but the authority may shorten this term in favour of those who request it, alleging and proving services to the Republic.

Article 17° .- Every Lutinian citizen is obliged to arm himself in defence of the homeland and of this Constitution, in accordance with the laws enacted for this purpose by Congress and the decrees of the National Executive. Citizens by naturalisation are free to render or not this service for a term of three months from the day on which they obtain their citizenship card.

Article 18° .- The people neither deliberate nor govern except through their representatives and the authorities created by this Constitution. Any armed force or gathering of persons claiming the rights of the people and petitioning in the name of the people commits the crime of sedition.

Article 19° .- In the event of internal commotion or external attack which endangers the exercise of this Constitution and of the authorities created by it, the province or territory in which the disturbance of order exists shall be declared in a state of siege, and constitutional guarantees shall be suspended there. During this suspension, however, the President of the Republic may not himself convict or impose penalties. His power shall be limited in such a case with respect to persons, to arrest them or transfer them from one point of the Nation to another, if they do not prefer to leave Lutinex territory.

Article 20° .- Congress shall promote the reform of existing legislation in all its branches, and the establishment of trial by jury.

Article 21° .- The principles, guarantees and rights recognised in the above articles may not be altered by the laws regulating their exercise.

Article 22° .- Congress may not grant the national Executive, nor the provincial Legislatures to the provincial governors, extraordinary powers, nor the sum of public power, nor grant them submissions or supremacy by which the life, honour or fortunes of the Lutinian people are placed at the mercy of any government or person. Acts of this nature carry with them an irrevocable nullity, and will subject those who formulate, consent or sign them, to the responsibility and penalty of the infamous traitors to the homeland.

Article 23° .- The Constitution may be amended in whole or in any part thereof. The necessity of amendment shall be declared by the Congress by a vote of at least two thirds of its members; but it shall not be effected except by a Convention called for the purpose.

Article 24° .- This Constitution, the laws of the Nation which may be passed by Congress in consequence thereof, and treaties with foreign powers are the supreme law of the Nation; and the authorities of each province are bound to conform to it, notwithstanding any provision to the contrary contained in provincial laws or constitutions.

Article 25° .- The Federal Congress shall make no law abridging the freedom of the press or establishing federal jurisdiction over it.

Article 26° .- The declarations, rights and guarantees enumerated in the Constitution shall not be understood as negating other rights and guarantees not enumerated; but which arise from the principle of the sovereignty of the people and the republican form of government.

Article 27° .- Judges of the federal courts may not at the same time be judges of the provincial courts, nor may the federal service, whether civil or military, give residence in the province in which it is exercised, other than that of the habitual domicile of the employee, this being understood for the purposes of eligibility for employment in the province in which they happen to be.

Article 28° .- The names adopted successively from 24/03/2022 to the present, namely: Lutinex Republic, United Provinces of Lutinex, Lutinex Kingdom, Kingdom of the United Provinces of Lutinex, Federal Kingdom of the United Provinces of Lutinex, shall henceforth be official names indistinctly for the designation of the Government and territory of the provinces, the words 'Lutinex Nation' being used in the formation and sanctioning of laws.
