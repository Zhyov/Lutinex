CONSTITUTION OF THE LUTINEX NATION

PREAMBLE

I, the creator and King of the Lutinex Nation, wrote this text, which is inspired on the Argentinian Constitution, with the objective of: having a unified nation, having justice in our country, having peace in our country, having freedom in our country, to live even better and to protect each other. This Constitution is for every Lutinian and for all people who want to live in our country.

FIRST PART

Chapter One

Declarations, rights and guarantees

Article 1° .- The Lutinex Nation is a Federal Talsampsic (Republican Representative with a seperate Absolute Monarch above the Representative President/s) Republic, this being the Kingdom of its United Provinces.

Article 2° .- The Lutinex Nation's official languages are: English, Ryabaj (written as Rjabázh), Spanish (written as Español) and Toxer (written as Tósker), English being the one used officialy inside the government.

Article 3° .- The capital of the country is the City of Uron in the province of Navioc. The national authorities live in the capital city.

Article 4° .- The national treasury is the money available to cover the national government's expenditures. The money in the National Treasury comes from: sale or rent of land in the country, taxes on things going in and out of the country, taxes paid by the population and loans that the government borrows when the money from taxes is not enough. Taxes should be equal: the more you have, the more you pay.

Article 5° .- Each province has its own constitution. Provincial constitutions must: respect the representative and republican form of government, respect what the national constitution says, ensure their own judicial power and organise their municipal system. The national government ensures each province the exercise of its own institutions.

Article 6° .- The national government may intervene in a province to: secure the republican form of government, repel external attack, ensure that authorities threatened or expelled by rebellion or invasion from another province are restored to office.

Article 7° .- The acts of government of one province are valid in all provinces. The judicial proceedings of one province are valid in all provinces.

Article 8° .- Citizens of one province are citizens in the other provinces. Citizens of one province have the same rights as citizens of other provinces. A person arrested in one province must be handed over to the authorities of another province which claims him or her for trial.

Article 9° .- In the Lutinex Nation there are customs offices at the borders of the country. The national Congress creates the taxes that are charged on the country's borders.

Article 10° .- Goods can move freely within the country. Goods can be manufactured in the country or brought from other countries. Goods and animals moved from one province to another are not taxed.

Article 11° .- A new province may be formed by the union or division of other provinces. The legislative powers of those provinces must agree to the formation of the new province. The national Congress must agree to the formation of the new province.

Article 12° .- The people of this country have the right to: work, sail and trade, petition the government, walk freely throughout the country, enter and leave the country, stay in the country, say and publish what they think, use and decide what to do with their things, associate to do good and useful things, practise the religion of their choice, teach and learn.

Article 13° .- In the Lutinex Nation all persons are free. In the Lutinex Nation slavery is prohibited.

Article 14° .- In this country there are titles of nobility. We are all equal before the law apart from the creator and King of the nation, who is above it. We all have to pay taxes. We can all have a job according to our our abilities.

Article 15° .- Property is immutable. No one can restrict the use or take away our things unless: a judge decides it because a law says so or the government decides it because we have something necessary for everyone. The government must pay us if it takes something from us for the benefit of everyone. Every author or inventor owns his or her work for as long as the law says.

Article 16° .- For someone to convict you, there has to be a law that says that what you did is a crime. No one can arrest you without a judge's order. They cannot convict you without a trial. You have rights even if you are charged in a trial. You have the right to have a lawyer defend you in a trial. No one can force you to say things against you in a trial. No one can enter your home without your permission.

Article 17° .- One's private acts cannot be judged by judges unless they offend: public order, the common good, the rights of others. You may do anything that is not forbidden by law. You must not do anything forbidden by law.

Article 18° .- Foreigners living in our country have the right to: work, to own things, buy and sell things, to walk freely throughout the country, keep their religious beliefs, marry and make wills. Foreigners can be Lutinian citizens if they: live for one consecutive month in the country, or render services to the Lutinex Nation.

Article 19° .- We must all defend our country. We must all defend this Constitution. Our duty as Lutinians is to defend our country and this Constitution. The people who chose to be Lutinians are not obliged to defend our country.

Article 20° .- In our country only elected representatives of the people can govern. Any group of people or armed force that rebels against the government commits a crime.

Article 21° .- The government declares a state of siege when something very serious happens in a part of the country: an internal crisis or an attack from outside. The state of siege is to defend the constitution. The government can apply security measures during a state of siege. The government cannot apply sentences during a state of siege. A state of siege suspends some civil rights and guarantees. The government can arrest people or take them to another part of the country during a state of siege. People can leave the country if they do not want to be arrested or taken to another part of the country during a state of siege.

Article 22° .- The legislature is responsible for making and changing laws. The legislature has to establish trial by jury.

Article 23° .- Regulations of laws may not modify the rights recognised in this Constitution.

Article 24° .- The legislature cannot give the president more power than he already has. The legislatures of the provinces cannot give the governors of the provinces more power than they already have.

Article 25° .- The Constitution may be changed in whole or in parts. The need for changes to the Constitution must be declared by the national Congress with a special majority. The Constituent Convention is the body that will make changes to the Constitution.

Article 26° .- The most important laws in our country are: the Constitution, the laws passed by the national Congress and international treaties. Other laws that are created in the country must respect what the Constitution says.

Article 27° .- The National Congress may not make laws that restrict the freedom of the press.

Article 28° .- The Constitution lists declarations, rights and guarantees. There may be rights and guarantees that are not in the Constitution. Rights and guarantees that are not in the Constitution may arise from: the principle of sovereignty of the people or the republican form of government.

Article 29° .- Federal judges cannot be provincial judges. A federal government employee working in a province has no legal domicile in that province.

Article 30° .- Our country can be named: Lutinex Republic, United Provinces of Lutinex, Lutinex Kingdom, Kingdom of the United Provinces of Lutinex and Federal Kingdom of the United Provinces of Lutinex. In the laws, Lutinex Nation is used to name our country.
